Bee habitat

This picture reveals several beehives revealed inside a fallen tree. As deforestation and other forms of development continue to demolish trees and natural habitat, bees are left with no where to safely gather. This is causing their numbers to rapidly decline along with other factors.

Harmful pesticides

This picture shows a farmer spraying pesticides on the crops. While there are safer pesticides options, sadly harmful pesticides known as neonics have been used in high demand. Neonicotinoid pesticides can impact a bee's life and whole colony by affecting their grooming that protects against things like diseases and mites, navigation or direction, and can cause their returned pollen to contaminate the rest of the colonies pollen and food reserve that also makes what is our honey.

Did you know that bees can fly over 55,000 miles to make 1lb of honey and make 100lbs of honey in a year. They have an extraordinary sense of smell and use the sun to help them navigate. They have three types of bees in a colony: the queen, the workers, and the drones that work together.
